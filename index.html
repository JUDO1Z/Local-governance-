Social Services

As local governments are tasked with delivering essential services such as education, healthcare, sanitation, and water supply to their constituents. This includes the construction and management of schools, clinics, waste management systems, and other social amenities.

Economic Development

Local authorities promote economic growth and development by creating a conducive environment for business activities, attracting investment, and supporting local enterprises. They may implement policies and initiatives aimed at fostering entrepreneurship, job creation, and poverty reduction.

Environmental Management

Local governance in Ghana also involves efforts to protect the environment and natural resources within communities. This includes initiatives for waste management, pollution control, conservation of biodiversity, and sustainable land use planning.

Local governance in Koforidua, the capital city of Eastern Region, operates within the framework of the decentralized system established by the Local Government Act of 2016. Koforidua is part of the Eastern Region and serves as the economic, political, and cultural hub of Ghana. As such, local governance in Koforidua is of paramount importance in addressing the diverse needs of its large and rapidly growing population. One significant aspect of local governance in Ghana is the involvement of traditional authorities, such as chiefs and queen mothers, who play a crucial role in local administration, especially in rural areas. These traditional leaders often collaborate with elected officials to address community needs and resolve disputes. The Local Government Act of 2016 governs local governance in Ghana and outlines the roles, responsibilities, and powers of local government authorities. At the core of this system are the assemblies, which are composed of elected representatives known as assembly members, as well as government-appointed officials and traditional authorities.

Community Participation

One of the key principles of local governance in Ghana is the active involvement of citizens in decision-making processes. Assemblies hold regular meetings and consultations with community members to gather feedback, identify priorities, and involve them in the planning and implementation of development projects.

Infrastructure Development

Local authorities are responsible for the provision and maintenance of basic infrastructure such as roads, bridges, schools, and health facilities within their jurisdictions. They allocate resources and oversee the implementation of development projects to improve the quality of life for residents.

Revenue Generation and Management

Local governments in Ghana have the authority to mobilize revenue through various means, including property rates, business licenses, fees, and taxes. They are responsible for managing these finances efficiently and transparently to fund local development initiatives.
